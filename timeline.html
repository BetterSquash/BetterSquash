Timeline

Started April 2024 and updated as I discover new items of interest. It is absolutely a work-in-progress and if you have found your way here, well done, as I haven't published the link yet. I will colour code the different types of entries, i.e. Rules, Rackets, Balls, Courts, Books, and any other category I use.

At the bottom of the page, and linked to via reference numbers where appropriate, is a reference section. Let's be clear, I'll be taking ALL the details from books, websites and especially the World Squash Library, so make sure you follow the reference links if you want more details

  • Pre-Squash No date
    Squash Rackets (now called Squash) came from Rackets, which while it doesn't really come from an exact predecessor, can be traced back to Real Tennis and other games where people hit a ball, either with their hands or bats/rackets.
  • Birth Of Squash Rackets Late 1800s
    Generally said to have started in Harrow school, but Gerald Pawle, Squash Rackets, pg13, says "while other maintain that it began in the courts of Elstree school."
  • First Private Squash Court 1883
    Vernon Harcourt, a former Harrow pupil, built the first recorded private court in Oxford. 1
  • Ball and Court Colour Pre-1900
    Gerald Pawle, Squash Rackets, pg14, says "My own earliest games were played on a court which my grandfather built at Wildford Hertfordshire, before the beginning of the century. It had the usual stone floor and black walls. We used a pink ball..."
  • The Game Of Squash 1901
    The very first book about squash was published by Eustace Miles (Photograph), who BTW, was an Olympian and prolific author.
    What I find interesting is that he calls the sport "squash" not "squash racquets/rackets" as most others seem to have done around that time.
  • Court Construction Early 1900s
    Gerald Pawle, Squash Rackets, pg13, says "Many were unroofed, with uneven stone floors and rough walls.". So it's clear the early courts were essentially outdoor courts.'
