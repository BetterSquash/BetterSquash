SQUASH GLOSSARY

  • ACE:Originally, this term meant any winning shot, as opposed to a mistake from the other player, but nowadays, it's used to mean a winning serve that the opponent doesn't hit - just like in tennis. Unlike tennis though, an ace in squash is very unusual except at beginner and newcomer level.
  • ANGLE:An outdated term, that meant hitting the ball against the side wall before the front wall (what we now call a boast) but only when hit in front of the Short line.
  • BOAST:A shot that hits a side wall or back wall BEFORE it hits the front wall. There are sub-definitions of this shot, for example a trickle boast, (UK term). A boast can be a defensive or attacking shot. It's a shot that opens up a number of possibilities that other rackets sports like tennis or badminton don't have.
  • BOUNCE:When a ball hits the wall or floor. In squash, the second bounce is usually more important due to tactical and rules related reasons.
  • GET:Another way of saying retrieve, meaning to reach and hit the ball before it bounces twice that was very difficult to reach. This term is also used in tennis. Real-world example: "WOW, what a great get!"
  • NICK:The join between the floor and the side walls and back wall, but not the front wall. If the ball hits the nick at the right angle it will be a winner.
  • RALLY:A collection or combination of shots that start with a serve. While it is possible to have a "one-shot" rally, i.e. a winning serve (sometimes called an ace), it is unusal in squash. There is not maximum number of shots for a rally.
  • RETRIEVE:To reach and hit the ball before it bounces twice. This can also used as a noun: retrieval. Real-world examples: "Great retrieval!" and "How did he retrieve that?"
  • REVERSE ANGLE:A type of boast where the ball hits the side wall on the opposite side from the hand it has been played. For example, if a right-handed player hits a forehand that hits the right had side wall first, this is a normal boast, but if the shot hits the left hand side wall first, that would be a reverse angle shot. This is true for the backhand and for left-handed players.
  • ROLLER:This is a type of nick where the ball hits at the perfect angle and rolls along the floor giving the player no chance to retrieve it.

This is a collection of the words and phrases connected with the sport of squash. I hope you find it useful. If you believe I am missing a term, send your suggestion to SquashGlossary@gmail.com

I will not be including players, tournaments, equipment manufacturers or governing bodies in the glossary.
