Programme Details

How It Works

  1. Get Your Code: Receive a unique referral code when you sign up for any service (Video Analysis, Solo Routines, or Challenges).
  2. Share With Others: Pass your code to fellow squash players through email, social media, or word of mouth.
  3. They Save Money: When your referrals use your code at checkout, they receive a 10% discount on any service.
  4. You Earn Rewards: We track all successful referrals linked to your code and notify you when you reach reward milestones.

Reward Milestones

Referrals Your Reward
1 referral Your choice of a bonus Solo Routine or Challenge Group
3 referrals Your choice of a free Solo Routine Set or Challenge Set
6 referrals Free Video Analysis session
Every 3 additional referrals (9th, 12th, etc.) Another free Video Analysis session

Programme Terms

  • Code Usage: Referral codes must be entered during checkout. Discounts cannot be applied retroactively.
  • No Limits: You can refer unlimited people and share your code publicly on social media or forums.
  • All Services Count: Referrals count toward your total regardless of which service is purchased.
  • Repeat Purchases: Multiple purchases from the same person using your code all count as separate referrals.
  • Commercial Licenses: Referring someone who purchases a Commercial License counts as 2 referrals.
  • Self-Referrals: You may use your own code once for a 10% discount and receive credit for 1 referral.
  • No Expiration: Your referral achievements remain valid indefinitely.
  • Processing Time: Please allow up to 48 hours after reaching a milestone to receive your confirmation email.
  • Program Updates: We reserve the right to modify these terms with advance notice to participants.

Challenge Categories

Learn about how each category is different

Introduction

The challenges I supply are not simply "Do x number of shots in this area" or those trending "If you can do this many squats, you are stronger than 80% of the population"

I want each and every challenge to improve your squash in some way, not just test you. Read on to find out more.

Solo Drills

As you may know, I strongly believe in the power of solo drilling. No high-level squash player has reached their standard without spending dedicated time training alone on court.

In fact, I offer a dedicated programme called Solo Routines.

These challenges focus on subtle adjustments to how you perform the drill rather than aiming for specific targets or meeting strict quality standards.

Some challenges don’t include progressions because they focus on rhythm, control, and feel rather than strict targets or shot counts.

Pairs' Drills

Nearly every club player has practiced a pairs’ drill at some point. Unfortunately, it’s often treated as just a warm-up at the start of a session, reducing its value and impact.

Like the solo challenges, the pairs’ drill challenges focus on new ways to approach drills. However, unlike solo challenges, pairs’ drills often include quality control criteria.

My goal is to encourage players to think deeply about these drills rather than just memorising shot sequences.

These challenges progress from cooperative to competitive, and trust me—cooperative can be even tougher!

I see these challenges as a stepping stone toward a full Pairs’ Routines programme in 2026.

Condition Games

Condition games impose specific limitations on one or both players. They dictate where the ball can be hit and which shots are allowed.

The conditions develop excellence. When your shot options are limited, precision and quality become essential.

These challenges push you to adapt under pressure. The most common condition game, length only, sharpens your ability to hit straight and deep instead of playing unnecessary boasts.

Unlike other categories, these challenges serve as introductions to the concept rather than redefining how condition games are played.

Match Play

While these challenges may resemble Condition games, Match Play challenges are never disclosed to your opponent.

Additionally, they are typically applied to just a few points per game, unlike Condition games, which last the entire game.

Some challenges restrict your options in specific positions, while others compel you to adopt a particular style of play.

These challenges are great for playing against opponents who know your game and also encourage you to experiment with new tactics.

Mindset

In the past 20 years, amateur squash players have changed how they train. Specifically, they’ve incorporated more strength and agility training.

I believe today’s average competitive squash player is fitter and stronger than their pre-2000 counterpart. They began imitating professionals—and that’s a good thing.

Mindset training is the new strength training. Playing squash for fun doesn’t mean you can’t improve what happens between your ears!

These challenges will push you to approach certain mental aspects from a new perspective. Some may take you out of your comfort zone, while others will encourage deeper reflection.

Physical

First, I’m not a qualified fitness expert. These challenges aren’t particularly demanding, but use common sense—if something doesn’t feel right, stop.

This category features unusual exercises designed to add variety to your training.

You won’t find me asking you to hold wall sits for 3 minutes or do 100 press-ups a day. I hope at least 70% of these exercise challenges are new to you.

Some help prevent injuries, while others strengthen neglected areas.
