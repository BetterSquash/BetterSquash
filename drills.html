INTRODUCTION

A "drill" is practice of a single shot or combination of shots. A "routine" is a combination of drills. Going onto court with no plan or idea of what you should do can be a waste of time.

Having fixed-time routines makes your time on court more effective, more fun and makes the time go faster. Try one of the routines below and see the benefit.

Each routine has a downloadable poster and associated video.

THE ESSENTIALS: SPR-01

OVERVIEW
This is a general solo skills routine that works on accuracy and power. Features a repeated core drill of side-to-side followed by 5 different drills, packaged into a mega set routine.

BENEFITS
- Improves accuracy.
- Improves power.
- Improves swing and timing.
- Improves fitness.

PREREQUISITES & TARGET AUDIENCE
- Good club-player standard
- Watched and practiced the side-to-side video and able to do at least 10 with no mistakes.

TIME REQUIRED
20 to 60 mins per Mega set (adjust according to your standard and goals)

THE CHECKERBOARD CHALLENGE: SPR-06

OVERVIEW
Uses the most basic solo hitting drill, but adds various challenges. This routine combines skill AND concentration practice.

BENEFITS
- Greatly improves the most fundamental ball control.
- Improves understanding of the relationship between frontwall height and hitting speed.

PREREQUISITES & TARGET AUDIENCE
- Improver standard and above.
- Able to hit 10 shots off the frontwall with no mistakes.
- Final progressions are suitable for very advanced players only.

TIME REQUIRED
16 mins per full court (adjust according to your standard and goals)

THE RAMY H

OVERVIEW
You hit the ball around the court in the shape of an H. It's much much harder to do than it looks.

BENEFITS
- Improves rackethead and ball control.
- It's a lot of fun.
- Allows you to impress your coach, family, friends and opponents!

PREREQUISITES & TARGET AUDIENCE
- The ability to keep working hard until you have achieved something.
- Good club standard or be very lucky when you mishit the ball.

TIME REQUIRED
As long as you want to do it for!

THE DEEP FIGURE OF EIGHT

OVERVIEW
Standing behind the service boxes in the middle of the court, you hit the ball to the frontwall and it comes back to you

BENEFITS
- Improves ball control and understanding of angles.
- Helps get your core and shoulders stronger.
- Great for buoilding a rhythm.

PREREQUISITES & TARGET AUDIENCE
- The ability to standard "figure of eight".
- Good club standard or be very lucky when you mishit the ball.

TIME REQUIRED
No more than 10 minutes.
