SQUASH GLOSSARY

  • ACE: A winning serve the opponent doesn’t touch — rare in squash except at beginner level. Previously used more broadly to mean any winning shot.
  • ANGLE: An outdated term for a shot hitting the side wall before the front wall, similar to a boast, but only when struck in front of the short line.
  • BOAST: A shot that hits a side or back wall before the front wall. Includes variations like the trickle boast. Can be defensive or attacking.
  • BOUNCE: Any time the ball touches the floor or wall. The second bounce is especially important due to rules and positioning.
  • NICK: The junction between floor and side/back walls. A well-placed shot landing here is almost always a winner.
  • RALLY: A sequence of shots starting with a serve. Can consist of one shot (like an ace) or dozens. No maximum length.
  • RETRIEVE: To return a shot before the second bounce. Also a noun: “That was an amazing retrieval!”
  • REVERSE ANGLE: A boast that hits the opposite side wall from the playing hand before the front wall. Adds tactical variety.
  • ROLLER: A perfect nick where the ball rolls along the floor, making it impossible to retrieve.

This is a collection of words and phrases used in squash. I hope you find it useful. If I’ve missed one, email: SquashGlossary@gmail.com
